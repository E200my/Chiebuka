Understanding human biology in fitness and nutrition

20.06.2024 4 minutes read

Introduction
Ever wondered how your body crushes that workout or uses food for fuel? How your muscles tend to enlarge despite the pains after a tedious work out? Then continue reading as you are about to satisfy your curiosity about human biology, fitness and nutrition . What a synergistic concept! The body and it’s organs can not function without adequate diets containing the right and balanced amount of nutrients like the carbohydrates, proteins and fat which we call the “Macro Trio” and the vitamins and minerals which although are needed in small quantities are very essential for the body’s protective functions which includes prevention of diseases and boosting of the immune system. Keeping fit which is a very important to the body and well being of an individual is all about maintaining a healthy physical condition through a combination of exercise and healthy eating. It goes beyond just looking good, although that can be a great benefit, but extends to an individual’s physical, mental and emotional well being.

Physical benefits cuts across:

  1. Improved Cardiovascular Health where Regular exercise strengthens the heart and lungs, allowing them to deliver oxygen more efficiently throughout the body. This reduces your risk of heart disease, stroke, and high blood pressure.
  2. Nutritional status: Biomarkers can reveal deficiencies in certain nutrients or help assess the effectiveness of a particular dietary plan.
  3. Weight Management: Keeping fit helps you maintain a healthy weight or lose excess body fat. This reduces the risk of obesity-related diseases like diabetes and some cancers.
  4. Stronger Bones and Joints: Exercise helps maintain bone density and improves joint flexibility, reducing the risk of osteoporosis and injuries.
  5. Boosted Immune System: Regular physical activity can strengthen your immune system, making you less susceptible to illnesses.


The mental and emotional benefits includes:
  1. Stress Reduction: Exercise is a great way to manage stress and anxiety. Physical activity releases endorphins, hormones that have mood-boosting and pain-relieving effects.
  2. Improved Sleep: Regular exercise can promote better sleep quality, leaving you feeling more refreshed and energized.
  3. Increased Confidence: Reaching fitness goals and feeling physically stronger can significantly boost your self-esteem and confidence.
  4. Sharper Mind: Studies suggest that exercise can improve cognitive function, memory, and focus.

Conclusion:
To fully understand the human biology in fitness and nutrition; First to start with is understanding that your body can not function without food because food supplies energy to do work and where there is no energy then there would not be any capacity to work. Just as a car needs fuel to work; so does the food we eat provide the fuel in form of energy to do the activities we want including exercising and keeping fit. Foods that contains carbohydrates e.g yam, rice, corn, potatoes, beans, spaghetti, amala, pounded yam, fufu etc are rich sources of energy; proteinous foods are eggs, soya, meat, milk, fish, edible insects e.t.c and lipids(fat/oil) can be gotten from nuts, sunflower oil, soya oil (please avoid animal fat). Through the consumption of protein which is responsible for building up and maintaining the tissues of the body and muscles help the muscles to repair it’s worn-out tissues which occurred during fitness training at the muscle fibre. During physical activity or training which makes the fibers of the muscles to contract as a result of the interaction of two proteins called actin and myosin. This contraction cause a microscopic tear in the fibers of the muscles but over time as the muscles fibre repairs the muscles get bigger and enlarged in size- this is why the muscles grow bigger as a person involved in constant exercise and fitness training. Hence, you can not erase nutrition from the picture if you are interested in building a healthy physique. This is also about creating a healthy and sustainable lifestyle that benefits your entire being. It is also having the energy to do the things you love, feeling good about yourself, and reducing your risk of chronic diseases. This is an investment in your long-term health and happiness. By understanding these concepts, you can make informed choices about exercise and food to reach your fitness and nutrition goals. Remember, your body is an amazing machine!

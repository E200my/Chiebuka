Exercise and Mental Health

20.06.2024 4 minutes read

Introduction
Mental health refers to a person's overall psychological and emotional well-being. It encompasses the way individuals think, feel, and behave, as well as their ability to cope with stress, manage emotions, maintain relationships, and function effectively in daily life. Good mental health is important for several reasons, as it affects every aspect of a person's life and overall well-being. Do you know that exercise plays a very crucial role in your mental health? Now, keeping a good mental health is a multidimensional concept that encompasses emotional, psychological, and social well-being; that is why it is good to prioritize mental health and the easiest way to do that is to involve in some physical activities that will help keep the mind sound. Good mental health contributes to a higher quality of life, enabling individuals to experience greater happiness, fulfillment, and satisfaction in their daily lives. Positive mental well-being enhances overall life satisfaction and subjective well-being. It is also a protective factor against the development of mental health disorders and illnesses. Building resilience, practicing self-care, seeking support, and maintaining healthy lifestyle habits can help prevent or mitigate the onset of mental health problems. In addition, good mental health fosters resilience, the ability to bounce back from adversity, cope with stress, and adapt to life's challenges. Resilient individuals are better equipped to navigate difficult situations, overcome obstacles, and maintain a positive outlook, even in the face of adversity and that is why exercise is always a good practice to boost mental health. There are various types of exercises and activities that can have positive effects on mental health by reducing stress, improving mood, and enhancing general wellbeing . Here are some types of exercises for mental health. Yoga combines physical postures, breathing techniques, and meditation to promote relaxation, stress reduction, and emotional balance. Practicing yoga can help calm the mind, reduce rumination, and enhance mindfulness, leading to improved mental well-being. Aerobic or cardiovascular exercises, such as walking, running, cycling, swimming, dancing, or aerobics, are effective for improving mood and reducing symptoms of anxiety and depression. 30 minutes of these exercises at moderate intensity is a good way to go. Strength training exercises, such as lifting weights or using resistance bands, not only improve physical strength and endurance but also have positive effects on mental health. Strength training increases levels of serotonin and dopamine, neurotransmitters associated with mood regulation and stress reduction. Engaging in sports, recreational activities, or team sports can as well be enjoyable ways to stay active and socialize with others, which can have positive effects on mental health. Whether it's playing basketball, soccer, tennis, or joining a recreational league, find activities that you enjoy and can participate in regularly.
Exercise as an anti- depressant
Studies has shown that exercise can treat mild to moderate depression as effectively as antidepressant medication—but without the side-effects. A recent research carried out in Harvard school of public health has confirmed that running for 15 minutes a day or walking for an hour reduces the risk of major depression by 26%.
This is possible to be because exercise releases endorphins, a powerful chemicals in the brain that energizes the spirit and make you feel good by promoting all kinds of changes in the brain, including neural growth, reduced inflammation, and new activity patterns that promote feelings of calm and well-being. Exercising is also a better way to kill anxiety and reduce stress.

Tips on getting started with exercises

A recent research carried out in Harvard school of public health has confirmed that running for 15 minutes a day or walking for an hour reduces the risk of major depression by 26%.

  1. Start Slowly: If you are new to exercise or returning after a period of inactivity, start slowly and gradually increase the intensity, duration, and frequency of your workouts. Begin with shorter sessions of low-intensity exercise and gradually build up as your fitness level improves.
  2. Choose Enjoyable Activities: Select exercises and activities that you enjoy and look forward to doing. Whether it is walking, cycling, swimming, dancing, or playing a sport, incorporating activities that you find enjoyable increases the likelihood of sticking with your exercise routine long-term.
  3. Find a Workout partner: Consider exercising with a friend, family member, or workout buddy for added motivation, support, and accountability. Having a workout partner can make exercise more enjoyable and help you stay committed to your goals.
  4. Start with Small Steps: Break down your exercise goals into smaller, manageable steps to avoid feeling overwhelmed. Begin by incorporating short bouts of physical activity into your daily routine, such as taking the stairs instead of the elevator or going for a short walk during your lunch break.
  5. Be Flexible: Be flexible and adaptable with your exercise routine, especially during busy or stressful times. If you're unable to stick to your planned workout schedule, find alternative ways to stay active, such as doing a quick home workout or practicing mindfulness meditation.
  6. Listen to Your Body: Pay attention to how your body feels during and after exercise. If you experience pain, discomfort, or fatigue, take a break and rest as needed. Pushing through pain can lead to injuries and setbacks, so prioritize safety and listen to your body's signals.
  7. Celebrate Progress: Acknowledge and celebrate your progress along the way, no matter how small. Whether it's reaching a fitness milestone, improving your endurance, or noticing positive changes in your mood and energy levels, celebrate your achievements and use them as motivation to keep moving forward.
  8. Seek Professional Guidance: Consider seeking guidance from a certified fitness professional, such as a personal trainer or exercise physiologist, to help you develop a safe and effective exercise program tailored to your individual needs, goals, and fitness level.
  9. Finally be Patient and Persistent; remember that improving mental health through exercise is a gradual process that takes time and consistency. Be patient with yourself and stay persistent, even on days when you don't feel motivated. Focus on the positive impact that exercise has on your mental well-being and keep moving forward one step at a time.
